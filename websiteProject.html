Career Interests and Goals

Education and Professional Experience

I have 15 years of experience as a Controls Engineer, a role I first started in after getting an AAS in Electrical Power & Controls from Terra State CC in 2011. This degree covered subjects such as:

  • Electrical Systems
  • Electronics
  • Process Control
  • PLC Programming
  • Mechanical Systems
  • Introductory courses in Computer Science
In the years since graduating I have gone through a great deal of on the job training, additional academic courses, and self-led MOOC's; where I learned robotics, new programming languages, networking principles, and database applications and more.

Throughout my career, the largest challenges have been tasks that fall more into CS than industrial automation. While these topics were part of the curriculum, my project assignments quickly surpassed the subjects I had learned in school. Instruction and direction from senior staff was helpful in getting through specific tasks, but it was apparent that I needed to seek additional training outside of work.

Interests and Goals

I have been interested in getting a Bachelors degree for many years since first graduating from Terra, both to gain new skills and to open up more career opportunities. There are many academic paths to get into or progress within my field of work, so it took quite a bit of contemplation to pick a major. Based on my years of experience, I was more interested in cross-training than getting a further degree in electrical engineering. Most applications I build are based on chemical process control or mechanical systems, so chemical and mechanical engineering were high on my list of considerations. However, I have always had one or more of these engineers on my team for every project so I questioned the need for myself to have specialized knowledge in these fields.

The right answer was fairly intuitive, as I have been independently studying Computer Science for many years now. I find these subjects to be fun, challenging, and incredibly useful. I tend to always be working on or thinking about a new tech project, and in the field of OT, nearly every CS subject has improved my professional abilities. I look forward to having more formal credentials and hope to work on more advanced systems with a deeper focus on software development.
